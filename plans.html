ola! To our Fit friends who are looking forward to the last couple of weeks of this month, packed with parties & outings, so on few occasions when you are at home, its advisable to eat something light & healthy.
For mealtime, we just adore loading up on leafy green salads combined with colorful vegetables, fruits & sometimes a sprinkling of nuts & cheese.
A Caesar Salad is often termed as the evil of all salads to order at a restaurant because of its high-calorie content & yet it’s one ordered by most.
A traditional Caesar salad is loaded with raw eggs, cheese, olive oil & mayonnaise. So here we present to you a healthy version of a Light Caesar Salad Dressing with homemade garlic croutons.
After trying this you just might wonder why you ate the heavier version at all! So cheers to a very Happy Year-End.

Ingredients


· ½ cup Greek style yogurt
· 2 tbsp low fat mayonnaise {Indian brands like Fun Foods are apt}
· Approx 2tbsp fresh lemon juice {can increase or decrease as per taste}
· 4 cloves garlic {grated or minced}
· 2 tsp Worcestershire sauce
· 1 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
· ¼ cup grated parmesan cheese
· 1 large iceberg or Romanian lettuce head, roughly torn by hand in bite-size pieces
· ¾ -1 tbsp Dijon mustard {can adjust to taste}
· A generous pinch of black pepper
· Salt to taste
· ½ cup of homemade croutons made with the bread of your choice [the bread should preferably be 2-3 days old to make crunchy croutons – Recipe given below]
· 200 gms grilled boneless chicken cut into bite size pieces (optional)

Method


· To make the Light Caesar Dressing, combine and whisk yogurt, mayonnaise, garlic, Worcestershire sauce, olive oil & Dijon mustard. Add the lemon juice at the end as per taste & whisk.
· If you feel the dressing is thick, add 1-2 tsp of water & whisk again. Ensure that it does not become too watery.
· Season with salt & pepper as per taste
· For the home made garlic croutons, the bread should be preferably 2-3 days old as it makes crunchier croutons. Preheat the oven to 350 degree F. Cut or roughly break the bread into small pieces. Put it on a baking tray or wire rack. Drizzle the bread with olive oil & garlic. Gently toss the croutons to ensure they are coated properly. Bake for 15-20 min or until golden brown.
